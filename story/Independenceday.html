Colombia Independence Journey

1781 - Revolt of the Communards:

The Comuneros rose up against the oppressive tax policies and other unpopular measures implemented by the Spanish colonial. The most prominent leader of the Comuneros was Juan Francisco Berbeo administration.
Place: Guaduas, Cundinamarca

1810 - The Exclamation of Independence:

A discontent between Creoles and the representatives of the Spanish crown led to the Creole leaders expressing their discontent and declaring their intention to form an independent government.
Place: Museo de la Independencia - Casa del Florero, Bogota

1817 - Policarpa Salavarrieta is Executed:

"Indolent people, I woman, perish for having wanted to set you free." by La Pola


Place: La Pola - Policarpa Salavarrieta Monument, Bogota

1819 - Boyaca's Battle:

The independence army strategically surprised the Spanish, achieving victory at Pantano de Vargas, Boyaca. Simon Bolivar's leadership played a crucial role.
